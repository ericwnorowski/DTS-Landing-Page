About Us

Philosophy

DTS Goalkeeping focuses on preparation in four different aspects of the goalkeepers game. The foundation of good goalkeeping starts with technical perfection. Each repitition needs to be focused on with detail and attention because there is always room for technical improvement. The second focus is tactics, an expansion of technique. Goalkeepers must have discussions and think through game-like scenarios to improve soccer IQ. The third is game preparation, keepers are put into a unique position because they need to come up with saves throughout the game. DTS makes sure keepers feel comfortable stepping into their boots on game day. And finally training for the mental side of the game because the a positive and confident attitude is intrinsic to their play.

Technical Development

There is not a single goalkeeper in the world, not even professionals that have perfect technique. High level goalkeepers are constantly looking to improve their technique through repition and experimentation. New techniques are emerging constantly from different corners of the world. DTS looks to push modern goalkeeping techniques to young keepers so they can properly develop.

Tactical Awareness

As important as technical sessions are, DTS feels that with each technical session, a tactical discussion around the topic must follow. Getting young keepers to understand the importance of each action and developing general soccer IQ is a major key to long-term success. DTS utilizes a variety of resources including video, demonstration, game film and more in order for keepers to visualize this information.

Game Preparation

One of the unfortunate parts of goalkeeping is game-time opporunities may not come frequently. Therefore when keepers step onto the field they need to feel confident in their abilities. DTS helps to develop and instill this confidence by demonstrating pre-game warm up routines, routinely practicing distribution and commmunication, and encouraging fearlessness when in goal.

Keeper Mentality

Sometimes called the lonliest job in sports, goalkeepers are known to have a different mindset than the average soccer player. Often times we are throwing our bodies in the way of shots, diving in the mud and just exemplifying grit. DTS understands that not all keepers naturally have these abilities, therefore we feel it is important to create a training atmosphere that encourages this type of mentality.

About DTS Goalkeeping

Eric Wnorowski

DTS Goalkeeping is headed by current Vassar College goalkeeper Eric Wnorowski. A former Wall Soccer Club and Christian Brother Academy GK, he has valuable and recent experience with NJ academy/club soccer, high school soccer, and now college. As an all-conference, all-state, and two-time state champion he is ready to help younger Shore Conference keepers elevate their game. Eric has worked with young keepers on complete game development as well as elite high school keepers on techincal training and college advice. Eric has worked with a number of coaches/programs in the greater Jersey Shore Area including SSTNJ, NJSSC Goalkeeping and Quickstrike FC.
